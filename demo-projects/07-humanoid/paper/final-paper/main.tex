\section{Preliminary}
\label{sec:introduction}
Oncology clinical trials operate around adverse events. Severity
matters, time to response matters, and the audit trail matters. The Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events
(\textsc{ctcae}) grading scale gives a common vocabulary across
sponsors, sites, and regulators \cite{ctcae_v5_2017}. The 2025 update
to the International Council for Harmonisation good clinical practice
guideline \textsc{ich e6(r3)} sets the modern compliance baseline
\cite{ich_e6r3_2025}. The April 2026 announcement from the U.S. Food
and Drug Administration of the Real Time Clinical Trial pilot (RTCT)
introduced a fresh 1 hour service level agreement for the intake of
grade 2 or higher adverse event records \cite{fda_rtct_2026,
fda2026realtime}. The United States has an obligation to lead on
patient safety, efficacy, and speed across the new field of
physical artificial intelligence for oncology trial
surgical procedures and supportive care.
